The following information is provided by a member of the Trans Victorian Forum reflecting their first-hand experience navigating the process

This information applies to those who are 19+ and have a BC birth certificate

Hopefully this will help some folks who wish to update their name and gender at the same time. While TransCareBC has their own guide, I found some of the steps were unclear and some of the information was quite confusing.

What follows are just my (cleaned up) notes from the many hours of research I did when I changed my name and gender legally in June of 2022 in Victoria. I have done my best to confirm that this information is still current as of July 2023, but nothing here is legal advice, so if you’re unsure, please double check with the specific organization for the step you’re at.

Updating BC Documents

You can update your name and gender at the same time. Use the following steps to submit both changes at once and have your new BC Birth Certificate issued.

Note: These are the steps that were recommended when I called Vital Statistics in June 2022. When I was dropping my documents off, the staff at my Service BC office were not aware that it was possible to submit both together.

  1. 1. Print off both of the following forms and fill them out:
    1. Adult Application for Change of Name (VSA 529A)
    2. Application for Change of Gender Designation (Adult and Minors Aged 12 Years and Over) (VSA 509A)
  2. 2. Make 2 photo copies each of your driver's license and your birth certificate.
  3. 3. Get fingerprints taken within the 30 days prior to submitting your forms.
    1. If you’re in Victoria, I recommend using Commissionaires in Victoria. I was able to book an appointment for the same afternoon as my Service BC appointment, and just went straight from one to the other.
    2. They will likely ask for your full new name, so be sure that you have fully decided before you go
  4. 4. Create two separate packages, one for each change (and then a copy of everything for your records):
    1. Change of Name
      1. Original (or most current) BC Birth Certificate
      2. Copy of receipt for payment of fingerprinting/criminal record check
      3. Photocopy of driver’s license (with current address)
      4. Notarized completed Adult Application for Change of Name (VSA 529A)
        • They offered to notarize the document for me at the Saanich Service BC office, although all the documentation states they only do that at the Vancouver office, so your mileage may vary with this.
    2. Change of Gender
      1. Photocopy of BC Birth Certificate
        • Ensure to write on this photocopied page that you have also submitted a name change form on the same date and ask that they hold off creating a new birth certificate until the name change has been completed (this is because a gender change is processed faster at a different office)
      2. Photocopy of driver’s license (with current address
      3. Completed Application for Change of Gender Designation (Adult and Minors Aged 12 Years and Over) (VSA 509A)
    3. Copies of everything for yourself
      1. Copy of birth certificate
      2. Original receipt of payment for fingerprinting
      3. Copy of completed name change form
      4. Copy of completed change of gender form
      5. Copy of completed change of name form
  5. 5. Take both packages into a Service BC office.
    1. I would recommend booking an appointment in advance.
    2. You can pay for everything in person at the Service BC office, and the forms should arrive at Vital Statistics the following day.
  6. 6. Wait for your Certificate of Change of Name and new Birth Certificate to arrive in the mail (up to 8 weeks; mine only took 4 weeks in June 2022)

    7. Once you receive your new documents you can proceed to the next steps:

  7. 7. Update Health Insurance BC (MSP)**
    1. Submit copy of your updated birth certificate and name change form to Health Insurance BC. This can be done online (I submitted the Change of Name Cert for the name change document and my Birth Cert for the gender document).
    2. You will not receive confirmation that your record has been updated. I had to call them to double-check (1-800-663-7100), but sometimes a doctor’s office or other health practitioner can check for you. Their site says it can take up to 21 days, and I called after 3 weeks and confirmed it was processed.
  8. 8. Update your BC Driver’s Licence
    1. If you have a combined BC Driver’s Licence and Services Card, ensure you have confirmed that your MSP has been updated before proceeding to update your BCDL
    2. Take your Change of Name Cert and Birth Cert to ICBC to apply for a replacement Driver’s Licence. I recommend booking an appointment ahead of time.
    3. More info

**2023-09-11: I have been informed that there is a new process in place, however it’s not officially documented anywhere, so you may want to confirm this yourself with ICBC.

You do not need to update your information with Health Insurance BC (HIBC) first if you have a combined card. Once you have your updated Birth Certificate, you can bring it, along with your Certificate of Name Change and 2 pieces of ID, to an ICBC and they will do it all in one step for you. They will scan your documents and send copies to HIBC for you. If HIBC needs anything else they will just contact you directly.

If you have a combined card and choose to separate them, the Driver's License will take the normal few weeks timeframe, and the standalone Services Card will be an additional 3-4 weeks following that to allow for the HIBC processing time. If you choose to do the combined card, it will be the same wait as the standalone Services Card.

Updating Federal Documents

  • Passport
    1. Complete the Adult General Passport Application Form
    2. Bring passport, proof of new ID, and new photos
    3. More info
  • SIN
    1. Can update online.
    2. No new SIN card is sent.
  • CRA
    1. Call them at 1-800-959-8281
    2. You may be able to update over the phone, if you have not changed any information with the CRA previously.
      • Note: I was unable to do this, as I had previously submitted my taxes with my middle name and then without it, which counted as a “name change” in their systems.
    3. If you are unable to update over the phone, you will need to mail or fax a letter with specific information laid out in the link below.
      • Note: If you are unable to access a fax machine, they require the original or a certified true copy of your Change of Name Certificate.
    4. More info
  • Elections Canada
    1. call 1-800-463-6868 or fill out the online contact form to request an Update Request form
    2. Complete and submit the form
      • As of June 2022, the form could be submitted via fax or mail (with copies of original)

Costs for updating Name and Gender

  • $27 - Gender Amendment Fee
  • $27 - New birth certificate
  • $137 - Change of Name Fee
  • $17 - Notarization fee (at Service BC office; may be more elsewhere)
  • $89 - Fingerprint fee at Commissionaires
  • BCDL will be either:
    • $17 - if not close to a renewal date
    • $75 - if your current licence is expiring within 180 days (6 months)
  • $120 (5 yr) or $160 (10 yr) (plus any cost for getting photos) - Passport

Other places to update your name and/or gender

This is not a complete list, as everyone’s list will be a little different, but I’ve included this as a starting point.

  • Any banks and other financial institutions you deal with
    • Once you update your name with your bank and on any credit/debit cards you have, you will need to update most of your automatic payments and saved information with any vendors (eg. Google Wallet, Amazon, etc)
  • Your workplace (for payroll, benefits, etc)
    • Even if you’re already out and have changed your name at work, there are likely certain things that they won’t be able to update until the legal change has happened (payroll, benefits, etc).
    • Make sure to ask if you need to update some things yourself. For example, I had to update my Pension Plan and my Union myself separately.
  • If you own property, you will need to update your Land Title.
    • Note: My condo insurance provider required the updated State of Title Certificate, so make sure you request that as well
  • Car and other insurance
  • Cell phone provider
  • Any utilities (internet, hydro, etc)
  • Public Library
  • Any membership cards (Costco, etc)

If you want help with your name change beyond the guide above, the Trans ID Clinic is a free local non-profit which helps trans people with their name changes and often cost. The events often occur at Island Sexual Health, the Central Library downtown, or in the UVIC student sub. Their Facebook page has the most up-to-date information available for new Trans Name Change ID events! Click here to visit the Trans ID Clinic's facebook page.

QChat provides a province-wide free, confidential and inclusive peer-to-peer support service for 2SLGBTQIA+ people, and a resource referral service. Their service welcomes all identities. They focus on 2SLGBTQIA+ youth ages 12-30, and are also happy to support people who are questioning if they might be a particular 2SLGBTQIA+ identity, people who want to learn more about the 2SLGBTQIA+ community or how to support someone from it, and older 2SLGBTQIA+ people.

Their peer support is provided by trained 2SLGBTQIA+ volunteers who complete a 5-week training program. It includes certification in the BC Peer Connect learning modules and completion of QChat's in-house training modules, developed in consultation with people who have lived experience and learned knowledge of the topics.

Common reasons why 2SLGBTQIA+ youth and their families reach out to QChat are to discuss relationships with friends and family, coming out, dealing with discrimination and learning about resources that can help. Basically, you can reach out any time you want someone to talk to, and Qchat is just as happy to help celebrate successes as help with challenges! Their website can be found here.
